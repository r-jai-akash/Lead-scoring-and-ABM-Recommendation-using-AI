Q1. What is the difference between supervised and unsupervised machine learning?. Which of the following statements are true?

a) Supervised learning is used when we want to predict a certain outcome from a given input.
b) The goal for unsupervised learning is to model the underlying structure or distribution in the data.
c) There are two major types of supervised learning problems, called clustering and regression.
d) Supervised learning problems can be grouped into clustering and association.

Correct answer : a) & b)


Q2. Select which of the following scenarios are regression problems.

a) Given a tweet, determine whether or not it contains text against or on favor for a presidential candidate.
b) Predict whether a user will churn from the service.
c) Predict how much a company will spend on electricity the next semester.
d) Predict the score that a student will achieve in an exam whose grade can be 0.1, 2,. . . , 10

Correct answer : c) & d)


Q3. Select which of the following scenarios are classification problems.

a) Predict the prices of a house in Boston based on zipcode, neighbourhood, the per capita crime rate by town, etc
b) An algorithm is trained to recognize spam email by learning the characteristics of what constitutes spam vs non-spam email.
c) Determine whether a customer is likely to purchase more items or not
d) Impact of blood alcohol content on coordination

Correct answer : b) & c)


Q4. Suppose you want to develop a supervised machine learning model to predict whether a superhero will fly or not. Which of the following statements are true?

a) This is not a machine learning problem
b) We'll use unlabeled examples to train the model.
c) A classification model provide the best approach.
d) A regression model is the best way to predict the probability to fly.

Correct answer : c)
